Having looked at the Burning Glass data, has your opinion of your ideal job changed? Why or why not?

When looking into the Burning Glass data recorded between March 2017 to February 2018 it is safe to say that the general skills in the greatest demand have not changed very much over the last few years, as skills of communication, problem solving, organisational skills, and teamwork are still the main top in-demand attributes sought by employers today (Labour Market Insights 2022). However, when looking into the specialised skills of IT the data shown in 2017-2018 reveals that SQL, JavaScript, Microsoft Windows, and Java were the greatest in demand, in comparison to today, Java, SQL and related database skills are still in high demand, but cloud computing and network related skills are emerging as more sought-after skills, which is something the group should take notice of.


Has the teams opinion changed on their ideal job? Why or why not?

The overall consensus of the group’s ideal jobs is that everyone's opinion on their desired field and future work has not changed, as for many of the group such as James, Jason T and Austin they all have work that requires desirable employee I.T skills from employers. However, this doesn’t mean I.T skills aren’t relevant to Jason D and Tommy, digital integration and I.T skill demand are progressively increasing within all industries (National Skills Commission 2021) and by upskilling and keeping up to date on industry technologies, systems, services and the technical skills required to effectively use them would allow us to be advantageous and excel in our positions.
