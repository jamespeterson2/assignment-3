Overall Group Reflection

Throughout our group discussion and the process of the assignment, we found that we were all able to work well together in a team. Everyone in our team contributed an equal amount of work towards the assignment by assigning tasks to meet everyone's key strengths and if anyone was stuck, there would be others in the team to help each other out. We all came to our meetings on time with full attendance e which allowed us to not waste any time in terms of preparation for the assignment but to get more work done. Group discussions were useful for us as they allowed us to find something else to work on if we were finished with our assigned tasks. Overall, the group worked extremely well together and was able to put together a well-constructed web page.

During our group’s reflection we discussed key areas that we feel needed to be improved for this assignment and going forward into assignment 3. We all felt that nothing outstandingly bad was occurring in terms of our group’s performance and assignment, however, we collectively agreed that on occasion during our group discussions there needed to be more initiative from group members in starting conversation based on their work. We believe that everyone needs to attain some level of leadership skills and confidence within their life and not be too afraid to speak up or lead conversations within groups. Furthermore, we realised that recordings during our group meetings were not being recorded, leading us to missing recordings and transcripts for many of our discussions. To fix this issue for the upcoming assignment we are making sure to enable automatic recordings by accessing the “meeting options” within teams, and visually verifying that the recording is active.

The main thing that really surprised all of us is how good all our potential job prospects are going to be in the future with everyone one of our ideal jobs having at least stable growth over the next five years. The biggest surprises were the increase in the projected employment rate for the cyber security analyst and the software developer roles with a 38.9% and 27% increase over the next five years respectively. Another surprise to our group was just how well we all worked together. Everyone attended every meeting we had, completed all the work they said they would by the time it was expected to be finished. This is likely because we scheduled our meetings a week before they happened to give everybody plenty of time. We also kept each other accountable for completing our sections and helped each other out if it was required.

The biggest thing that we learnt is that to be truly successful as a group we need to identify the biggest strengths of each group member and create an environment where everyone can work to their biggest strengths which allows for any weaknesses to be less exaggerated. In our group, we assigned the IT technologies sections to people whose fields encompassed the different topics chosen, James oversaw the creation of the initial website design as he had the most experience with HTML & CSS. Operating in this manner aloud for our group to function effectively is something we will look to implement in projects we are in during university.
